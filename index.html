About Acupuncture

Acupuncture is an ancient system of medicine used in China for thousands of years.

History

Acupuncture is an ancient system of medicine used in China for thousands of years. It is a safe and effective form of medicine. It was introduced to Europe in the nineteenth century. It is now used extensively throughout the West, with over two million treatments given in the UK each year.

Treatment Method

Chinese medicine looks at pain and illness as signs that the body is out of balance. The overall aim of acupuncture treatment is to restore the body’s equilibrium. By inserting fine needles into specific points on the body, shifts are made in the overall condition of the patient’s health and well being. The points used are determined by a careful and detailed gathering of information regarding symptoms and medical history as well as such things as sleeping patterns,appetite and digestion and emotional well being.

Point Selection

The point selection may be modified in subsequent treatments as the symptoms start to shift. The Chinese medical model talks about these shifts in terms of moving energy and changing patterns. This is simply referring to a return to a more relaxed, invigorated and healthier functioning of the body.



How does it work?

How does it work?

  • There are numerous theories about how acupuncture works. Some of these are:
  • Acupuncture stimulates the release of pain-relieving endorphins.
  • Acupuncture influences the release of neuro transmitters, substances that transmit nerve impulses to the brain.
  • Acupuncture influences the autonomic nervous system.
  • Research exploring a number of possible mechanisms for acupuncture effects is ongoing.

Treatment Frequency

Many people turn to acupuncture for help with a specific symptom or condition. Others choose to have treatment to help maintain good health, or improve their general sense of well being. Sometimes all that is required are a couple of treatments for a sore knee or ankle. Other times with more chronic conditions a longer term treatment plan is needed with appropriate goal setting and reevaluation points. Clients generally enjoy the experience of relaxing with the needles and love the way they feel after the treatment, describing it as both calm and invigorated.
Often clients choose to have treatment as part of their ongoing health maintenance at regular intervals.
